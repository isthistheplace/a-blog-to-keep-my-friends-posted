Yet more evidence Columbia students are lame

And a brief discussion of boredom

It was only a matter of time before this blog became a data blog. Who am I to resist fate?

This weekend, I was casually going through NY Open Data (as I often do when I'm bored), and I came across a cool dataset. This dataset estimates how frequently people travel from certain subway stations to others, averaged by the hour of the day and month. So, I decided to try to figure out where Columbia students go.

Among the most frequent criticisms that I heard about students were the following: people don't get off of campus and when they do, they often go downtown and not into Harlem, the Bronx, or any area that is not lower Manhattan. These data can't perfectly address either of these criticisms, but maybe they can shed light on each.

It's not meant to be perfectly informative, but hopefully it's lightly illustrative.
